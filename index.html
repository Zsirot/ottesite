Welcome


If you’re reading this I want to encourage you in your search for a therapist, making that first call to a therapist is one of the most difficult and hopeful things you can do about your situation.


My specialty areas are depression and addictions. I’ve been a practicing psychologist for over 24 years and I love the work. My focus in the last few years has shifted to a more spiritual orientation; this is due to working with people who are in recovery, and also relates to my own journey. Spiritual based psychotherapy is not about religion, but about keeping an open mind.


The truth about psychotherapy is that it works; people do get better. I work from a variety of orientations including Family Systems, Jungian theory, CBT, and mindfulness. It’s my job to help you take the energy that impelled you to make that phone call and begin the healing process.


– John A Otte | PsyD

Three Fundamentals

Spirituality

Spirituality is not religion

For me, spirituality is the acceptance of mystery; that there is an undefinable Power that is unseen and incomprehensible to the human mind, and this Power is completely present and active in daily life. Spirituality in psychotherapy means an acceptance that the Power we can’t understand – which I call love – is most present in relationships, therapy included.

Depression

Like addiction, is also a disease of body, mind, and spirit.

Depression talks to us and tells us that the negative ideas we carry about ourselves are true; they are our reality when we are depressed. Our thinking and core beliefs need to change, the shame and guilt we carry be transformed. And, again, this happens through a renewal of the spirit. As in all solid psychotherapy, this can be very painful and difficult. But people do get well.

Addiction

Addiction, as opposed to a bad habit, is a disease of the body, mind, and spirit.

As a friend of mine says, it’s a whole person disease. There’s no one way, there are a thousand paths to healing, health, and wellbeing. There is a great paradox to the cure of addiction, it is only through acceptance, surrender, and love that we get well.
